Books and Films

Read... Read.... Read...

Being able to read books (and see films) is really good and must be a part of our self-education.

Here is a list of books and films that I have read or seen and that I recommend you to read or see.

Books

1984

Mentionned in the Hobbies pages, 1984 is a Dystopian book made by Georges Orwell

In this universe, the world is divided by three big powers :

  • Eastasia
  • Oceania
  • Eastasia

And the main character, Winston Smith, is a worker in the Ministry of Truth, where he is in charge of rewriting the history of the world.

Between destructions, war, Big Brothers and love, this book is the representation of the imagination of Georges Orwell.

You can safely click on the picture, I promise.

But, when we look about the French Professor of Georges Orwell, we found Aldous Huxley.

And M.Huxley write also a dystopian book, called

Brave New World

Did you see this transition !?

Brave New World is a dystopian book made by Aldous Huxley

In this universe, there is international state.

The humanity is classed under 5 castes :

  • The Alphas, the elites of the Humanity, tall and smart
  • The Betas, the smart workers
  • The Gammas, the working class
  • The Deltas, small, unintelligent, programmed to make the manual work.
  • The Epsilons, how the f*** they live !?

Each class is sub-divided by two classes, the plus and the minus class.

In this eugenic world, we follow the story of Bernard Marx, an Alpha plus with some deformation due to some alchool in his test-tube.

You can safely click on the picture, I promise.

Even if 1984 and Brave New World are big classics in the dystopian world, this duality between "the old world" and the over-technologied world represents, I think, our world today at some place in this world.

Films

Interstellar by Christopher Nolan

Do not go gentle into that good night... Old age should burn and rave at the close of day... Rage, rage against the dying of the light...

Interstellar is definitly, one of the most beutiful film in the 21th century.

It's just so hard to explain why but i'll try to make a list of argument

  • The story is excellent, Nolan is well known to be litteraly, an artist and a specialist of storymaking.
  • THE MUSIC ! OH MY F****** GOD ! Almost all the musics in this film are maded by Hans Zimmer, the mix between classical, modern and THE ORGAN is just arrgh!
  • From the scientist point of view, almost all the theory, the calculous, the images have been verified by Kip Thorne, a (just) nobel price of Physics (yeah, just that.)

And the last argument, the most important, the most beautiful, the most... Interstellar is a film about the human, about the love, about the family, about the life. (and the time)

You can (and you must) review this film, again, again and again !

Before clicking on the poster, get an pair of headphones and enjoy :D

Dreams are messages from the deep.

Frank Herbert !
